# Welcome to cyberdeck.club beta! 🛠️

Hey y'all — thanks for being here. Seriously. You're among the first people to poke around cyberdeck.club, and that means a lot.

I'm building this platform because I wanted a space where cyberdeck builders — especially women, femmes, queer folks, beginners, and anyone who's ever been told "that's not how it's done" — could share what they're making without having to prove they belong first. You belong here before you do anything.

Whether your build is a Polly Pocket with a tiny computer inside, a vintage clamshell compact, or a souped-up Pelican case — it counts. There are no gatekeepers here.

This post walks you through what's here, how beta works, and how you can help shape what this becomes.

---

## 🗺️ What's here (and where to find it)

cyberdeck.club has four main sections:

- **[Wiki](/wiki/)** — Community-written articles about cyberdeck building. Techniques, component guides, inspiration boards. If you have maker-level access, you can create and edit pages.
- **[Forum](/forum/)** — Where conversations happen. Ask questions, share ideas, help each other out. No question is too basic.
- **[Builds](/builds/)** — A gallery of cyberdeck builds from the community. You can submit your own build to share it — new members' builds go through a short review to keep things safe and welcoming, then publish automatically after that.
- **[Meetups](/meetups/)** — Community events, both online and in person.

You can find the full community guidelines at **[/guidelines](/guidelines/)**. The short version: celebrate creativity (not specs), give constructive feedback, respect pronouns and names, and remember that this space was built for folks who've been excluded from other maker communities.

---

## ⚠️ How beta works (and what's different)

A few things to know about this beta environment:

- **This is a testing ground.** Some of the user accounts here are test accounts used during development. Not every profile you see is a real person (yet!).
- **There's a risk of data loss.** I may need to reset the database, roll back changes, or wipe content as I fix bugs and ship updates. Don't put anything here you'd be heartbroken to lose — production (the real, permanent site) will be the place for that.
- **Things might break.** That's expected! If something looks wrong or feels off, that's exactly the kind of thing I want to hear about.

---

## 💬 How to share feedback

There's a **💬 button in the bottom-right corner of every page**. Tap it, describe what you noticed, and it'll automatically grab a screenshot and create a tracked issue on GitHub. You don't need a GitHub account — the feedback widget handles everything.

You can view all the feedback you've submitted at **[/my-feedback](/my-feedback/)**.

That's the fastest way to report bugs, suggest features, or flag anything that feels confusing or unwelcoming. Even small things ("this button label confused me") are genuinely helpful.

## 🗓️ How to see the roadmap

If you want to see what's being worked on, what's coming next, or where your feedback ended up, the roadmap lives in a public GitHub Project:

👉 **[cyberdeck.club roadmap](https://github.com/orgs/cyberdeck-club/projects/1)**

You can see what's in progress, what's planned, and what's been shipped. Feedback you submit through the widget gets tracked there too.

---

## 🔄 How often I'll circle back on feedback

I'm a single engineer building this, so here's what to expect:

- **During beta:** Faster feedback loops. I'll be reading everything that comes in and shipping fixes and changes frequently — often within days. This is the time when your input has the biggest impact on the direction of the platform.
- **After beta:** Longer cycles between updates, but I'll still be reading every piece of feedback. I'll post updates in the forum when batches of changes ship.

If you submit something and don't hear back right away, it's not lost — it's tracked. I'll get to it.

## 🤖 AI tools and your creative process

Our stance on AI is part of the [community guidelines](/guidelines/) — the short version is that using AI tools is an individual decision, and we don't police how you make things. Shaming someone for using or not using AI tools will get you banned. Check the full guidelines for details.
